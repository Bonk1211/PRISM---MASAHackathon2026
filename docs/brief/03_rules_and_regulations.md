# 3. Rules & Regulations

## Registration

- Participants must register as part of a team via the official registration platform by the deadline announced on MASA's official channels.
- The MASA Hackathon is open to **all undergraduate students only**.
- Teams must consist of **three to five (3–5) members**. Each individual may join only one team, and registration is non-transferable.
- By registering, participants agree to comply with these Terms & Conditions and all event guidelines.

---

## Participation

- **Registration:** Teams are required to fill in an online registration form (URL: https://forms.gle/BaKzEJWaQ1sZD4HG8), no later than **11:59 p.m. on 23rd April 2026**. The form will be made available at 12:00 p.m. on 3rd April 2026. Any appeal for an extension of the registration period will not be entertained.
- **Problem Statement Release:** The problem statement and dataset will be released by **12:00 p.m. on 24th April 2026 (Friday)**.
- **Final Entry Deadline:** Final submissions must be attached to a completed entry submission form ("Submission Form") no later than **11:59 p.m. on 7th May 2026 (Thursday)**.
- **Late Submissions:** Submissions received after the Entry Due Date are ineligible for participation in the Hackathon.

---

## Submission Guidelines

- **Submission Channel:** Submissions will be accepted via the entry submission form (URL: https://masahackathon2026.vercel.app).
- **Entry Limit:** Each Team is allowed to submit only one Entry in this Hackathon.
- **Multiple Submissions:** If a Team submits more than one Entry, the latest submission received prior to the Entry Due Date will be judged.
- **Originality:** A Team's submission and all supporting work must be completed solely by the Team. All submissions must be the original work of the Team and created in response to this Hackathon. Submissions found in part or entirety to be other than original work created for this Hackathon will be disqualified.

---

## Submission Criteria

### Preliminary Round Requirements

#### → Report Format and Specifications

- **Cover Page:** The cover of the submitted report must state the Team's name, names of each Team member, and their respective university(s).
- **Language:** Submissions must be in English.
- **Length Constraints:** The body of the report's length cannot exceed **10 pages**. If the body of the report exceeds 10 pages, only the first 10 pages will be considered.
- **Formatting Requirements:** Reports must have **1-inch (2.5-cm) margins** and a **12-point font size**. Reports with narrower margins or smaller font sizes will be downgraded during judging.
- **Page Limit Exclusions:** The cover page, table of contents, bibliography, and appendix/appendices will not count toward the 10-page limit.
- **File Format:** The submitted report must be a single document in **PDF (.pdf)** format.
- **File Size:** The report file must be a maximum of **100 megabytes**.

#### → Source Code Specifications

- File Format for R: `.R` or `.Rmd`
- File Format for Python: `.py` or `.ipynb`
- **Comment Requirements:** The submitted code file must include comments to explain code chunks.
- **README** file with setup & replication instructions.

#### → Appendices and Supporting Documents

- Optional Materials.
- Appendices and supporting documents are optional; **up to three** are allowed.
- Appendices and supporting documents must be submitted in one document or file.
- Each Entry will be comprised of the entry submission form plus a maximum of **four files**. Each file must be a maximum of **100 megabytes**. Files larger than 100 megabytes will not be entertained.
