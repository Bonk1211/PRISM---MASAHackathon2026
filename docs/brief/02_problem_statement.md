# 2. Problem Statement — Climate Risk Assessment

## Background

In the dynamic field of Actuarial Science, data analytics and statistical modelling are integral to understanding and mitigating emerging risks. The demand for precise predictive modelling emphasizes the importance of staying updated with advancements, especially regarding global challenges like climate change. A range of programming languages and analytical platforms are used in actuarial analytics and **R** is among the most popular due to its open-source nature, strong community support and flexibility.

The hackathon focuses on equipping participants with practical knowledge of analytical tools including, but not limited to, **R and Python**, enabling them to handle complex socio-economic and environmental datasets and derive actionable insights for long-term financial resilience. By integrating analytical tools mentioned above into actuarial problem-solving, participants will be better prepared to meet industry demands in climate-risk assessment.

---

## Challenge

You are engaged as an **actuarial consultant** to advise a **multinational reinsurance firm** exploring how climate-related risks may impact its long-term financial resilience. The client has asked you to perform an independent strategic assessment using the **World Bank's World Development Indicators (WDI)**.

> ⚠️ **Please download the "Wide format" data.**

**Dataset Source:**
- World Bank Group — Data 360
- World Development Indicators (WDI)
- 1,513 Indicators | 1960–2024 | 217 Economies
- Source: World Bank (WB)
- Download: Wide format

---

## Key Considerations

- **Indicator Identification:** Identify and discuss environmental, energy or socio-economic indicators that are most informative for understanding climate-related risks. Highlight and justify significant relationships or patterns.

- **Predictive Modelling:** Construct a model to predict greenhouse gas emissions (or another climate-related indicator you consider relevant) for **2024**. Discuss your assumptions, pre-processing steps, validation methods, and reasoning for the selected model. Explain the implications of your model.

- **Insurance Claims Analysis:** Explain the relationships between climate indicators and natural disaster insurance claims. Given that the client has regional exposure in **Southeast Asia**, compare the trends in natural disaster insurance claims between **two Southeast Asian countries**. Support your analysis using external research or open-source data.

- **Mitigation Strategy & Stress Test:** Propose a climate-related mitigation strategy related to the client. Using your baseline model as the reference point, conduct a **stress test** to project the impact of your selected strategy to your selected climate-related indicator by year **2030**.

- **Strategic Summary:** Summarise key insights for the client. Highlight limitations and uncertainties. Provide actionable recommendations for strategic risk management.

---

## Execution Process

Participants will work with the World Bank's World Development Indicators (WDI) "Wide format" dataset, alongside external research on natural disaster insurance claims. Throughout the hackathon, they will explore key data preprocessing techniques, including cleaning, transformation, and structuring of global climate and economic indicators using various data analytics tools and programming languages. Leveraging these techniques, participants will analyse the dataset to uncover meaningful patterns, develop predictive models, and perform stress testing to assess potential impacts of climate-related risks on insurance claims.

During the **Grand Final**, participants will present their solutions, supported by reliable external data, to a panel of judges. This process encourages both technical proficiency and strategic thinking in managing climate-related risks.
